Chronology

Key events, travels, and achievements of Frédéric Chopin

Year Event
1810 Born on 1 March in Żelazowa Wola, Poland, near Warsaw.
1817 Composed his first piece, a Polonaise in G minor, at age 7.
1826–1829 Studied at the Warsaw Conservatory under Józef Elsner, developing his distinctive Romantic style.
1829 Gave his first major public concert in Vienna, receiving enthusiastic reviews.
1830 Left Poland just before the November Uprising, eventually settling in Paris.
1831 Arrived in Paris and soon gained recognition among the leading musicians and aristocrats.
1835 Received French citizenship. Continued composing major works, including the Nocturnes and Ballades.
1837 Began his relationship with the novelist George Sand.
1838–1839 Stayed in Mallorca with Sand, where he wrote many of his Preludes (Op. 28).
1846 Health declined; wrote some of his most introspective late works, including the Cello Sonata.
1848 Toured England and Scotland, performing his last concerts due to his worsening tuberculosis.
1849 Died on 17 October in Paris at age 39. Buried in Père Lachaise Cemetery.
